Cookie Policy

Last Updated: May 10, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

2. How We Use Cookies

Dhruv Technology Solutions LLC uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to:

  • Ensure our website functions properly
  • Improve your browsing experience
  • Understand how visitors use our website
  • Protect against spam and abuse
  • Remember your preferences

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

Purpose: Required for the website to function properly

Duration: Session or persistent

Can be disabled: No (website won't work without them)

Examples:

  • Session management cookies
  • Security cookies
  • Load balancing cookies

3.2 Functional Cookies

Purpose: Remember your preferences and choices

Duration: Persistent (up to 1 year)

Can be disabled: Yes

Examples:

  • Language preferences
  • Cookie consent preferences
  • Form data retention

3.3 Analytics Cookies

Purpose: Help us understand how visitors use our website

Duration: Persistent (up to 2 years)

Can be disabled: Yes

Examples:

  • Vercel Analytics cookies
  • Page view tracking
  • User behavior analysis

6. How to Control Cookies

6.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept All: Allow all cookies
  • Reject Non-Essential: Only allow essential cookies
  • Customize: Choose which cookie categories to allow

6.2 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

Google Chrome:

  1. Click the three dots menu → Settings
  2. Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  3. Choose your preferred cookie settings

Mozilla Firefox:

  1. Click the menu button → Settings
  2. Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  3. Manage your cookie preferences

Safari:

  1. Safari menu → Preferences
  2. Privacy tab
  3. Manage cookies and website data

Microsoft Edge:

  1. Click the three dots menu → Settings
  2. Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data
  3. Manage and delete cookies

6.3 Opt-Out Links

You can opt out of specific third-party cookies:

  • Google: https://adssettings.google.com/
  • Network Advertising Initiative: http://optout.networkadvertising.org/
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: http://optout.aboutads.info/

7. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies, some features of our website may not function properly:

  • Essential cookies disabled: Website may not work at all
  • Functional cookies disabled: Preferences won't be saved
  • Analytics cookies disabled: We can't improve user experience

8. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Currently, there is no industry standard for how to respond to DNT signals. We do not currently respond to DNT browser signals, but we respect your cookie preferences set through our cookie banner.

9. Mobile Devices

Mobile devices may use identifiers similar to cookies. You can manage these through your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Tracking
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads

10. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legislation, or our business practices. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised "Last Updated" date.

11. Your Rights

Under GDPR and other privacy laws, you have rights regarding cookies:

  • Right to be informed: Know what cookies we use
  • Right to access: Request information about cookies
  • Right to withdraw consent: Change your cookie preferences anytime
  • Right to object: Object to certain cookie uses
